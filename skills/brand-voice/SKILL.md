Before drafting any public-facing copy:

1. **Find the voice** — look in Notes / Drive for a brand voice doc (names like "Brand voice", "Voice & tone", "Messaging"). If none exists, ask for 3–5 bullets: who we are, who we serve, words we use, words we avoid, and one example line that sounds like us.
2. **Never invent brand claims** — products, pricing, customer logos, metrics, or legal promises must come from the user or a cited source. Flag gaps instead of filling them in.
3. **Capture reusable lines** — when the user approves a strong line, offer to save it back into the brand voice note for next time.
4. **Stay consistent** — match register (casual vs formal), person (we/you), and punctuation habits from the voice doc.

Output shape for a voice capture:
- **Positioning** — one sentence
- **Audience** — who and what they care about
- **Tone** — 3 adjectives + 3 "we don't" rules
- **Signature phrases** — up to 5 approved lines
- **Avoid** — banned words or clichés
