---
name: Meeting notes format
description: How to structure meeting notes when the user asks for a summary of a call or meeting. Read before producing meeting minutes or summaries of discussions.
source: seed
---

When writing meeting notes or minutes:

1. Lead with **Decisions** — what was agreed, one line each, no discussion recap.
2. Then **Action items** — each with an owner and a due date (ask if unknown; never invent one).
3. Then **Discussion** — the key points and disagreements, in the order they mattered (not chronological order).
4. Close with **Open questions** — anything left unresolved, phrased so the next meeting can pick it up directly.

Style rules:
- Bullet points, not prose paragraphs.
- Names before verbs ("Dana to draft the proposal", not "the proposal will be drafted").
- Keep the whole document scannable in under a minute; cut anything that doesn't change what someone does next.
